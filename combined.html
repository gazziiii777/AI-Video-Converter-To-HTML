Product Definition and Key Features

The Prusa CORE One is a fully enclosed CoreXY 3D printer engineered for precise printing with multiple materials. The Prusa CORE One features a 250×220×270mm build volume within an all-steel exoskeleton frame. This frame provides structural rigidity while maintaining a compact footprint of 415×444×555mm. The enclosed chamber of the Prusa CORE One reaches temperatures up to 55°C, enabling successful printing of engineering-grade materials including ABS, ASA, PC, and Nylon without warping. Additionally, the automatic ventilation system of the Prusa CORE One allows for printing PLA and PETG with the door closed for safety and temperature stability.

The Prusa CORE One utilizes Prusa's Nextruder direct drive extruder with a 10:1 planetary gearbox ratio. This gearbox ratio delivers precise filament control and up to 30mm³/s flow rates through its high-flow CHT nozzle. The 360° cooling system on the Prusa CORE One enables printing of 75° overhangs without support structures, which saves material and reduces post-processing time. For temperature capabilities, the hotend of the Prusa CORE One reaches temperatures up to 290°C with the heated bed capable of 120°C. For calibration purposes, the Prusa CORE One incorporates a load cell sensor for automatic bed leveling and Z-offset calibration, eliminating manual adjustments typically required on other printers.

Regarding connectivity, the Prusa CORE One includes Ethernet, optional Wi-Fi module (included with purchase), and USB drive support. These connection options provide full compatibility with Prusa Connect for remote management and monitoring. For user interaction, the Prusa CORE One operates through a 3.5" color touchscreen with intuitive controls. For print quality, the Prusa CORE One includes factory-calibrated Input Shaper profiles that mitigate vibration artifacts at higher speeds. For long-term value, the modular construction of the Prusa CORE One allows complete disassembly with standard tools, supporting long-term maintenance and upgradability. This design approach extends to filament compatibility, as the Prusa CORE One works with the MMU3 multi-material upgrade for printing with up to five filaments simultaneously.

Which materials can you use with the Prusa CORE One?

The Prusa CORE One prints with 14 different filament types thanks to its enclosed design with active temperature control. The Prusa CORE One handles standard materials including Polylactic Acid (PLA), Polyethylene Terephthalate Glycol (PETG), and Thermoplastic Polyurethane (TPU). Beyond these standard materials, the Prusa CORE One also supports engineering materials including Polycarbonate (PC), Polypropylene (PP), Copolyester (CPE), Polyvinyl Butyral (PVB), Acrylonitrile Styrene Acrylate (ASA), Acrylonitrile Butadiene Styrene (ABS), High Impact Polystyrene (HIPS), Nylon (PA), and composite filaments with carbon fiber or metal particles.

The Prusa CORE One works with both Prusament and third-party materials. Prusament is Prusa's in-house filament brand. Each Prusament filament comes with a pre-tested profile in PrusaSlicer, eliminating the need for manual calibration. For third-party filaments, users of the Prusa CORE One can create custom profiles with their own settings. This open approach to materials gives users flexibility in choosing filaments from any manufacturer.

The hardware specifications of the Prusa CORE One enable broad material compatibility. The hotend of the Prusa CORE One reaches 290°C (554°F), making it capable of handling high-temperature materials like polycarbonate. The heated bed of the Prusa CORE One reaches 120°C (248°F), providing strong adhesion for technical filaments. For environmental control, the enclosed chamber of the Prusa CORE One maintains internal temperatures up to 55°C (131°F), preventing warping with ABS and ASA. For PLA and PETG printing, the automatic ventilation system with rear exhaust fans maintains lower chamber temperatures under 30°C, allowing these materials to print with the door closed for safety and consistency.

Different materials printed on the Prusa CORE One serve specific applications. PLA prints on the Prusa CORE One serve as decorative objects, prototypes, and basic functional parts. PETG creates durable functional components with minimal stringing thanks to the printer's temperature management system. For flexible applications, the direct drive Nextruder extruder system handles TPU with shore hardness values down to 40D/85A, making it suitable for cushioning and protective cases. When printing with engineering materials, ASA and ABS produce parts for outdoor applications and automotive components without warping due to the enclosed chamber. For maximum strength, PC and Nylon create engineering components requiring durability and heat resistance.

The standard 0.4mm brass Cluster Heatbreak Technology (CHT) nozzle on the Prusa CORE One works with non-abrasive materials. For more demanding applications, carbon fiber or metal-filled filaments require a hardened steel nozzle to prevent wear and extend nozzle life. This nozzle upgrade is available separately and expands the material capabilities of the Prusa CORE One for industrial and engineering applications.

PrusaSlicer offers precise control over printing speeds and temperatures through both simplified one-click printing for beginners and detailed settings for experienced users. This dual approach makes the software accessible regardless of user experience level.

What is the design and build quality of the Prusa CORE One?

The Prusa CORE One uses an all-steel exoskeleton design that provides structural rigidity and stability. The Prusa CORE One frame consists of precisely cut and folded sheet metal that eliminates bi-metallic effect issues found in printers mixing different metals with varying thermal expansion rates. This consistent material construction in the Prusa CORE One ensures print quality remains stable across temperature ranges.

The enclosure of the Prusa CORE One combines black steel panels with translucent polycarbonate panels for the door and upper sides. These polycarbonate panels resist cracking and shattering, unlike glass panels used in competing printers. For visual distinction, orange accents highlight key components against the black exterior, maintaining Prusa's signature color scheme.

Both side panels of the Prusa CORE One feature functional indents with specific purposes. The right side contains a recessed filament holder that keeps spools within the printer's footprint, reducing required space. The left side includes a storage recess for customizable organizational accessories. These indentations serve a dual purpose by reducing the internal chamber volume, allowing faster heating for high-temperature materials. This reduced volume is particularly beneficial when printing materials like ASA or ABS that require consistent chamber temperatures.

The door of the Prusa CORE One includes hinges that stop at both 90 and 180 degrees and can be configured to open from either left or right side based on workspace needs. For maintenance accessibility, all panels attach with removable plastic rivets, allowing complete disassembly with basic tools. This modular approach means no components are glued or welded in place. Due to this design decision, users can access any part of the printer for repairs or upgrades.

The Prusa CORE One uses a CoreXY motion system with a linear rail for the X-axis and 10mm smooth rods for the Y-axis, delivering precise movement while maintaining rigidity. For Z-axis movement, three lead screws control bed movement, providing stability during printing. All motion components mount directly to the steel frame, minimizing vibrations at higher print speeds. This rigid mounting approach helps maintain print quality even when printing at faster speeds.

Practical design elements in the Prusa CORE One include a magnetic touchscreen mount that prevents damage if the printer tilts forward. For visibility and status indication, LED lighting in the bed handle illuminates the print area and changes color to indicate printer status. For environmental control, the top panel features an adjustable vent slider for airflow management, while dual rear exhaust fans provide active temperature regulation. This ventilation system allows printing PLA with the door closed, unlike competitors that require open doors for low-temperature materials.

The entire Prusa CORE One is designed for serviceability with standard tools, ensuring long-term reliability and easy maintenance. This approach to serviceability aligns with Prusa's history of continuing support for their printer models through regular firmware updates and upgrade paths.

How reliable is the Prusa CORE One and what maintenance does it require?

Long-Term Reliability

The Prusa CORE One features an all-steel exoskeleton frame that provides stability during printing operations and minimizes vibrations. The solid construction maintains consistent performance even after extensive use. It incorporates proven components from the MK4S, already tested in Prusa's own print farm operations. For reliable extrusion, the Nextruder extruder uses a planetary gear mechanism that delivers consistent filament control without slippage. For precise motion, the CoreXY motion system combines linear rails and smooth rods that maintain dimensional accuracy with minimal adjustment after initial setup.

Maintenance Requirements

The Prusa CORE One requires regular cleaning of the build plate with appropriate solutions based on the sheet type. For cleaning, isopropyl alcohol works for smooth and textured sheets but damages the Nylon sheet. After 200 hours of printing, the X-axis linear rail needs inspection and cleaning with a clean paper tissue or cloth. When cleaning this component, the linear rail should never be treated with degreasers or chemicals that damage the rubber in the carriage. For proper function, lubrication of the linear rail is needed every 3-6 months using a dedicated linear rail lubrication set.

The Y-axis smooth rods require cleaning with a paper towel and lubrication with Prusa Lubricant after 200 hours. As part of ongoing maintenance, the belt tension needs checking after 500 hours of printing. For smooth vertical movement, Z-axis leadscrews require lubrication with Prusa Lubricant after 500 hours of print time. This lubrication process is simplified by using the Z thread greaser printed part available from Prusa's website. To prevent overheating, the cooling fans need inspection and cleaning every few hundred hours to remove dust and plastic debris. For this cleaning, compressed air works best for dust removal, while tweezers can remove plastic threads.

To ensure reliable electrical connections, screw-attached and Molex-attached connectors should be checked and reconnected after 600-800 hours of printing. Over time, the primary gear in the Nextruder can accumulate filament debris. This accumulation requires periodic cleaning through the access hole on the side of the extruder to maintain consistent extrusion.
